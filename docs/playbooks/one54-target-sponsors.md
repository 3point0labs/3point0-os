# One54 — Target Sponsor Playbook

**Purpose:** Living doc of categories One54 actively pitches and the angle for each. Read by the outreach agent before drafting any One54 email so the pitch matches the sponsor's category. Update as you learn what lands and what doesn't.

---

## How to use this file

When the agent drafts a One54 email, it should:
1. Identify which category below the sponsor falls into
2. Use the **Angle** for that category as the foundation of the pitch
3. Reference the specific audience signal that matches (from the audience interest profile in `one54-pitch.md`)
4. Avoid the listed pitfalls

If a sponsor doesn't fit any category cleanly, default to a "cultural moment" pitch leaning on the show's positioning and audience scale.

## Active target categories

### 1. Financial services / credit & lending (Tier S)

**Audience signal:** "Credit & Lending" is a top shopping behavior in the One54 audience. Diaspora professionals are actively in market for these products.

**Examples:** Chase, Capital One, Cash App, Zolve, Nala, Sendwave, Wise, Chime, SoFi

**Angle:** "Your next million customers are One54 listeners — first and second-generation diaspora professionals who are actively researching financial products as we speak. The audience interest data already says they're shopping. The question is which brand shows up in their feed first."

**What to lead with:** Audience shopping behavior data + reach numbers. This category responds to numbers, not vibes.

---

### 2. Streaming services / TV / entertainment platforms (Tier S)

**Audience signal:** "Streaming Services" is a top shopping behavior. Audience overlap with Comedy & Entertainment Fans interest profile.

**Examples:** Netflix, Hulu, Max, Paramount+, BET+, Apple TV+, Showmax, Amazon Prime Video

**Angle:** "Your platform's African and diaspora content needs the audience that actually watches it. One54's listeners aren't a future market — they're 220M views in 5 months telling you they're already here."

**What to lead with:** Specific shows or content the streamer has greenlit that align with the African diaspora story. Tie to a real recent slate decision.

---

### 3. Music & audio platforms (Tier S)

**Audience signal:** Rap & Hip Hop Fans + Comedy & Entertainment Fans + the show's iHeartPodcasts production lineage.

**Examples:** Apple Music, Spotify, Tidal, Amazon Music, Audible, Boomplay, Mdundo

**Angle:** "Hip hop and Afrobeats audiences live on One54 — and we're already inside the iHeartPodcasts ecosystem. Co-promotion + branded segments turn listening time into platform-stickiness."

**What to lead with:** A recent African or diaspora artist deal the platform has done. Link to "Club Shay Shay maker" for credibility.

---

### 4. Global brands entering / serving Africa & the diaspora (Tier S)

**Examples:** Coca-Cola Africa, Heineken / Star Lager, Nike, Adidas, Uber Africa, Bolt, Meta Africa partnerships, Google Africa programs

**Angle:** "Insider-led credibility for brands that need to show up right. One54 is a show the audience trusts — guests like Boris Kodjoe, Festus Ezeli, and Tiffany Haddish only sit down for platforms that respect the culture. Your brand becomes part of that trusted environment."

**What to lead with:** A specific recent campaign or product launch the brand has done in or for Africa/the diaspora. If they're getting it right, say so. If they're new to the conversation, position as their entry point.

---

### 5. Consumer / CPG African brands and African-founded brands (Tier A)

**Audience signal:** "Culturally driven · deeply loyal" audience profile. Diaspora professionals are the highest-value consumers for these brands.

**Examples:** 54 Thrones · Cavalli Coffee · Topicals · Bread Beauty · Uncle Nearest · Hutdesigns · Lively Root · diaspora wellness brands

**Angle:** "Your audience IS our audience. Diaspora professionals making intentional brand choices that connect them to home. One54 is the show that makes that buying decision feel like a cultural statement."

**What to lead with:** A specific product launch or audience the brand is trying to reach. Tier A means we don't lead with "Title Partner" pricing — Segment Sponsor or recurring features land better here.

---

### 6. Travel & hospitality (Tier A)

**Audience signal:** Diaspora professionals are high-spend on travel — both back to home countries and aspirational US/global travel.

**Examples:** Marriott, Hilton, Delta, Emirates, Ethiopian Airlines, Kenya Airways, RwandAir, Airbnb, Booking.com Africa programs

**Angle:** "The diaspora doesn't just take vacations — they take pilgrimages. Your brand becomes part of the journey home, or the journey out, and either way you're part of a conversation that has emotional weight no banner ad can match."

**What to lead with:** A specific route, destination, or partnership the brand has prioritized in Africa or for the diaspora.

---

### 7. Tech / SaaS for African markets and diaspora founders (Tier A)

**Audience signal:** "Business Professionals" is a core audience interest. Founders, operators, and decision-makers listen.

**Examples:** Flutterwave, Paystack, Chipper Cash, Jumia, Andela, Mono, Kuda, Bamboo, Piggyvest

**Angle:** "Decision-maker reach. The B2B audience for African fintech and SaaS lives inside this listenership. One54 doesn't sell you 'awareness' — it puts your product in the ears of the operators who buy it."

**What to lead with:** Recent product launch, funding round, or geographic expansion. B2B positioning, not consumer.

---

### 8. Sports brands with African ties (Tier B — not Tier S)

**Audience signal:** "Olympics / Sports Fans" is a top interest. Akbar's NFL background pulls a sports-curious audience even though sports is not the show's primary lane.

**Examples:** Nike basketball/Africa programs · NBA Africa · Premier League Africa partnerships · NFL Africa expansion · Wilson Africa programs

**Angle:** "Akbar Gbajabiamila is a former NFL player and Emmy-winning Ninja Warrior host. Sports brands with African DNA find natural integration here — but we lead with culture, not just the game."

**Why Tier B not S:** Hardcore sports buys go to Pressbox. One54 wins sports brands that want a *cultural* angle.

---

### 9. Education / professional development (Tier B)

**Examples:** Coursera, Masterclass, professional certification programs, business schools targeting diaspora applicants

**Angle:** "Audience aspiration is the through-line of this show. Education buyers reach diaspora professionals who already invest in growth and are receptive to credentials that travel internationally."

## Categories we do NOT pitch

- **Sports betting / fantasy gambling:** Wrong audience for One54. Send to Pressbox.
- **Crypto speculation, NFT projects, rug-pull-adjacent products:** Brand risk too high.
- **Payday lenders / predatory credit:** Hard no, regardless of category fit.
- **MLM / pyramid schemes:** Hard no.
- **Anything that treats the African diaspora as a charity case or development project:** This audience is sought-after consumers, not aid recipients. Brands that frame Africa as needing rescue get auto-rejected.

---

## Tier definitions

- **Tier S:** Dream sponsors. Drop everything to pitch. Title Partner or Presenting Sponsor scale ($100K+ deal range). Multi-episode, season, or live-event activation potential.
- **Tier A:** Strong fits. Pitch with intent and specificity. Segment Sponsor or multi-episode package range.
- **Tier B:** Decent fits. Volume-pitch in quarterly waves.

---

## Per-contact angles by title

- **CEO / Founder:** Mission + cultural moment. They respond to "the brands that show up first will own this relationship for a generation."
- **CMO / VP Marketing / Head of Brand:** Audience numbers + integration depth. Show the ladder from impressions to brand affinity.
- **Head of Partnerships / BD:** Lean into the iHeartPodcasts lineage and "Club Shay Shay maker" for institutional credibility. They know what those reference points mean.
- **Head of Multicultural / Cultural Strategy / Diaspora marketing:** Skip the explanation of why the audience matters. They already know. Lead with the specific integration — Segment Sponsor, Live Activation, custom campaign.
- **Comms / PR lead:** Earned-media narrative angle — guest appearance exchange, custom content collab.
