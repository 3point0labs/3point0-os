# Press Box Chronicles — Target Sponsor Playbook

**Purpose:** Living doc of categories Pressbox actively pitches and the angle for each. Read by the outreach agent before drafting any Pressbox email so the pitch matches the sponsor's category. Update as you learn what lands.

---

## How to use this file

When the agent drafts a Pressbox email, it should:
1. Identify which category below the sponsor falls into
2. Use the **Angle** for that category as the foundation of the pitch
3. Reference the specific audience signal that matches (from the audience interest profile in `pressbox-pitch.md`)
4. Avoid the listed pitfalls

If a sponsor doesn't fit any category cleanly, default to a "high-attention adult-male audience with documentary-grade content" pitch leaning on Pearlman's credibility.

## Active target categories

### 1. TV streaming services / sports media (Tier S)

**Audience signal:** "TV Streaming" is a top shopping interest. "Documentary & Nonfiction TV" is a VERY HIGH audience interest. The Hollywood Reporter literally called Pressbox "the new 30-for-30."

**Examples:** ESPN+, Peacock, Paramount+, Max, Hulu, Apple TV+, Netflix Sports, FuboTV, DAZN

**Angle:** "Pearlman tells the kind of stories your sports doc slate exists to find. Pressbox listeners are the audience for ESPN 30-for-30 reruns at 11pm. Your subscription product needs them. They're already shopping streaming as we speak."

**What to lead with:** A specific recent sports doc or scripted sports project the streamer has greenlit. If they've got new sports content launching, you're the perfect amplifier.

---

### 2. Credit cards / financial services / banking (Tier S)

**Audience signal:** "Credit & Lending" is a top shopping interest. The 45-64 male demo is the highest-value credit card and banking customer in the country.

**Examples:** Chase, American Express, Capital One, Discover, Citi, Bank of America, Wells Fargo, Marcus, SoFi

**Angle:** "Highest-LTV demographic in the country, attention spans of 7+ minutes per episode, returning every week. Your card / mortgage / loan product is in market with this exact audience. Pearlman's voice converts because the audience trusts him."

**What to lead with:** The shopping interest data + the demo. Financial services buyers respond to numbers.

### 3. Sports betting & fantasy (Tier S)

**Audience signal:** Engaged, attentive sports fans who watch the entire episode (54.4% completion, 7:13 view duration) — exactly the kind of bettor that converts beyond the impulse install.

**Examples:** DraftKings, FanDuel, BetMGM, Caesars, ESPN BET, PrizePicks, Underdog Fantasy

**Angle:** "Stop buying impressions from people who weren't going to bet anyway. Pressbox's audience watches sports their whole life. Pearlman host-reads land different because the audience trusts the voice. Branded segment > generic ad read."

**What to lead with:** A "Pressbox Prop of the Week" or "Story Behind the Spread" type integration concept. Differentiation from every other sports pod.

**Guardrails:** Responsible gaming language. Don't target states where not licensed.

### 4. Home purchase / mortgage / real estate (Tier S)

**Audience signal:** "Home Purchase" and "Mortgage" are listed shopping interests. The 45-64 male demo is buying second homes, refinancing, or helping kids buy first homes.

**Examples:** Rocket Mortgage, Better.com, LendingTree, Zillow, Redfin, Veterans United

**Angle:** "Direct overlap with your highest-value customer profile. The audience is in the buying / refinancing / inheritance-planning life stage right now. Pearlman's audio reads + custom promo codes drive measurable conversion."

**What to lead with:** Specific recent product launch, rate, or offering. Performance-marketing language lands here.

### 5. NIL platforms / athlete services (Tier A)

**Audience signal:** Hardcore sports fans who care about behind-the-scenes business of sports. Pearlman's reporting routinely covers contract, agent, and money stories.

**Examples:** Opendorse, INFLCR, MOGL, Athliance, Leverage, NIL collectives, agent CRM platforms

**Angle:** "We talk to athletes, agents, and the people moving sports money. Pressbox's audience IS your B2B audience for athletes considering your platform — coaches, parents, advisors. Branded segment > generic ad read."

**Marquel-specific lever:** Marquel is studying for the NFLPA Contract Advisor exam (July 2026). Pressbox sits closer to the agent world than any other sports pod we know of. Lean on this in pitches.

### 6. Athletic apparel / performance gear (Tier A)

**Audience signal:** Sports-fan adult-male demo with disposable income. Loyal returning audience = repeat exposure for brand-building.

### 8. Sports tech / recovery / nutrition (Tier B)

### 10. Sports media / team partnerships / co-promotion (Tier B — non-cash)

**Examples:** Meadowlark Media, Overtime, The Players' Tribune, team-owned media properties, regional sports media networks

## Categories we do NOT pitch

- **Africa-focused business products:** Wrong fit. Send to One54.
- **Unlicensed gambling operators:** Legal + brand risk.
- **Daily-fantasy products with lottery-style structures targeting casual bettors:** Reputational risk.
- **Supplement brands without NSF / Informed Sport certification:** Athletes can't use them safely.
- **Hot-take influencer media (Barstool-style):** Wrong tonal match.
- **Anything that disrespects the audience's intelligence:** Pearlman's brand is built on the opposite.

## Tier definitions

- **Tier S:** Dream sponsors. Drop everything to pitch. Presenting Sponsor or Custom Segment scale. Multi-episode or season-long potential.
- **Tier A:** Strong fits. Intentional pitch, personalized.
- **Tier B:** Decent fits. Volume-pitch in quarterly waves.

---

## Per-contact angles by title

- **CEO / Founder:** Mission + story. Sports execs respond to authenticity. Lead with why Pressbox is different (Pearlman + 30-for-30 framing) before reach numbers.
- **CMO / VP Marketing / Head of Brand:** Audience quality + storytelling integration. Lead with completion rate, return rate, and demographic LTV.
- **Head of Athlete Partnerships / NIL Lead:** Pitch as an athlete-adjacent storytelling channel, not a media buy.
- **Agent / Player Rep:** Episode guest exchange. Zero-dollar conversion that unlocks relationships downstream. Marquel's NFLPA path is the lever.
- **Sports law firm partner:** Authority play — branded segment on contract, NIL, or money-management questions.
- **Performance marketing buyer (mortgage, betting, fintech):** Numbers, numbers, numbers. Promo-code attribution lands here.
