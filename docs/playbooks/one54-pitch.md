## Sponsorship packages

- **Title Partner** — show naming rights ("One54 Africa Presented by [BRAND]"); pre/mid/post-roll on every episode (audio + YouTube); dedicated branded segment custom to your brand; full social amplification across show + host accounts; event/activation partnership rights. Host involvement based on availability/contractual obligations.
- **Presenting Sponsor** — category-exclusive; brand presence across all episodes; episode intros; pre-roll + mid-roll host-read ads (audio + YouTube); monthly social features across show accounts.
- **Segment Sponsor** — own a recurring moment inside every episode; product placement; segment-sponsored question or topic woven into the interview (your brand becomes part of the conversation).
- **Live Podcast / Event Activation** — live taping with real audience; collaborate on the experience and the brand integration.

## Positioning — what to say in a pitch

- **Do say:** "the first podcast built for the African diaspora at this scale," "$2.5 trillion consumer economy," "1.4 billion people," "iHeartPodcasts production, from the makers of Club Shay Shay," "220M+ views in 5 months from zero," "8M follower ecosystem before a single share," "deeply loyal, culturally driven audience"
- **Don't say:** "Africa is emerging" (cliché), "underserved market" (patronizing without context — use the data instead), generic reach claims without the specific number, anything that treats the audience as a niche
- **Always:** tie the sponsor's product to a specific audience interest (financial services → credit & lending behavior; streaming brands → existing streaming behavior; CPG → tastemaker culture). See `one54-target-sponsors.md` for category-specific angles.

## Voice & tone

- Confident, specific, data-led — never apologetic about the audience size or value
- The audience is not "emerging" — they are already here, already buying, and already shaping global culture
- Lead with the moment ("the brands that show up first will own this relationship for a generation")
- Talk to brands as cultural participants, not advertisers buying impressions

## Agent instructions (One54)

When drafting outreach, the agent should:
1. Open with one specific, recent reference to the sponsor company (from web search context provided)
2. Tie that reference to One54's audience using a concrete data point — never a generic "great fit"
3. Reference one of the proof points (220M views, 8M follower ecosystem, iHeartPodcasts lineage, specific guest, "Club Shay Shay" maker) when it lands naturally
4. Suggest one clear next step (15-min call, deck review)
5. Maximum 3 short paragraphs
6. Sign as Marquel Martin · 3point0 Labs

## Off-limits

- No predatory lenders or payday loan operators

- Nothing that treats the African diaspora as a charity case or development project — this audience is sought-after consumers, not aid recipients
